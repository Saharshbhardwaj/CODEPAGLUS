HINDU TEMPLES

1. Mahakaleshwar Jyotirlinga (Ujjain)

Significance:

  • One of the twelve Jyotirlingas (most sacred shrines of Lord Shiva).
  • Considered the holiest temple of Ujjain, located near the Kshipra River.
  • The only south-facing Jyotirlinga, believed to offer liberation from time and death.

Mythological Story:

  • Legends state that a demon named Dushana attacked Ujjain. The devotees of Shiva prayed, and Lord Shiva appeared as Mahakaleshwar to protect them.
  • Shiva stayed in Ujjain as a Jyotirlinga.

Cultural Relevance:

  • The ‘Bhasma Aarti’ is a unique ritual where the Shiva Linga is bathed in sacred ash before sunrise.
  • The temple is the focal point of the Kumbh Mela, held every 12 years in Ujjain.
  • On Mahashivaratri, the temple hosts a massive celebration, drawing devotees from across India.

2. Khajuraho Temples (Chhatarpur District)

Significance:

  • Built between the 9th and 11th centuries by the Chandela dynasty.
  • Famous for Nagara-style architecture and sensual sculptures symbolizing the unity of physical and spiritual life.
  • A UNESCO World Heritage Site, attracting international tourists and devotees.

Mythological Story:

  • According to legend, King Chandravarman was the son of the Moon God and a mortal woman. He built the temples to represent divine love and devotion.

Cultural Relevance:

  • The Khajuraho Dance Festival, held annually, showcases classical Indian dance forms.
  • The temples are dedicated to Shiva, Vishnu, and Devi (Goddess Durga & Parvati).

3. Omkareshwar Temple (Khandwa District)

Significance:

  • One of the twelve Jyotirlingas, located on an island shaped like the sacred ‘Om’ symbol in the Narmada River.
  • A major pilgrimage destination for Shaivites.

Mythological Story:

  • King Mandhata performed severe penance on this island, and Lord Shiva granted him divine darshan as Omkareshwar.

Cultural Relevance:

  • The temple is visited by thousands during Mahashivaratri and Kartik Purnima.
  • Devotees undertake the ‘Narmada Parikrama’, circumambulating the entire river as a religious vow.
